About


Welcome to my project website.

I am interested in harvesting infomation from remote sensing and social-economic datasets, using methods such as model optimization and machine learning, and eventually building models that explain and predict.

Most of the projects I have done so far are on the relationships among human activity, fire, vegetation and climate system, using remote sensing data and climate models.

Through these projects, I have acquired experiences working with Linux clusters, parallel computing, machine learning and model optimization.

I currently hold a B.S. in Ecology from Peking University and M.S. in Earth System Science from Univeristy of California Irvine. I also enjoy playing guitar and cello and doing photography when I have a chance.
